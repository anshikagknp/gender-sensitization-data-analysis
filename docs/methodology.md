# Research Methodology

## Research Design

The study used a **quantitative research methodology**, gathering data through a structured, closed-ended questionnaire administered via Google Forms. The instrument used multiple-choice questions designed to measure attitudes, beliefs, and awareness around gender issues. See `questionnaire.md` for the reconstructed instrument.

## Objectives

1. Explore and analyse perspectives on gender sensitization across demographic groups (age, gender, educational background).
2. Assess the current level of awareness and understanding of gender sensitization concepts among Kanpur's youth.
3. Provide actionable recommendations for improving gender sensitization training programs.

## Research Questions

1. What are the common perceptions and understandings of gender sensitization among different demographic groups?
2. What is the current level of awareness and knowledge about gender sensitization among the youth segment of the population?
3. What are the key recommendations for improving gender sensitization efforts in various settings?

## Hypotheses

- **H1:** Youth of Kanpur city (18–35 years) are fairly gender sensitized.
- **H2:** There is a lack of proper training or initiatives for gender sensitizing the youth of Kanpur city in schools and workplaces.

## Sampling

- **Sample size:** 100 respondents.
- **Sampling techniques:** A combination of **convenience sampling** (to efficiently reach an accessible young population) and **stratified sampling** (to ensure adequate representation of both men and women).
- **Distribution channels:** The survey link was shared via email, messaging services, and social media to maximize reach and response rate.
- **Target age range:** 18–35 years, chosen to capture a demographic seen as being at the forefront of social change.

## Sample Profile

| Category | Breakdown |
|---|---|
| Age | 18–25: 86% · 26–35: 10% · Below 18 / above 35: 4% |
| Gender | Male: 53% · Female: 47% |
| Education | High school or equivalent: 10% · Bachelor's degree: 66% · Master's degree: 24% · Doctoral/post-doctoral: 0% |

The LGBTQIA+ community was not represented in the sample, due to limited availability of respondents from that group — noted in the dissertation as a scope limitation.

## Data Analysis

Responses were downloaded from Google Forms and analyzed using **frequency distribution**, then presented as percentage breakdowns visualized primarily through pie charts (reproduced as tables in `key_findings.md`).

## Limitations

1. **Social desirability bias** — respondents may have given answers they believed were socially acceptable rather than their genuine views.
2. **Sample size and generalizability** — a sample of 100 limits how far findings can be generalized; a fully representative sample is inherently difficult to achieve.
3. **Measurement complexity** — gender sensitization spans a wide range of attitudes, beliefs, and behaviours that are hard to capture precisely through quantitative methods alone.
4. **Lack of qualitative depth** — quantitative methods may miss the underlying reasons behind attitudes; the dissertation recommends a mixed-methods approach for future research.
5. **Scope** — the study does not capture variation across cultural or temporal settings, and does not include the perspectives of LGBTQIA+ communities.
